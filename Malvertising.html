Introduction About Malvertising

What is Malvertising?

Malvertising is a type of online advertising that is used to distribute malicious software (malware) or to redirect users to malicious websites. Malvertising attacks work by exploiting vulnerabilities in the online advertising ecosystem, such as ad networks, to serve malicious ads to users. These ads may appear legitimate and may be displayed on reputable websites. When a user clicks on the ad or interacts with it in some way, they may be redirected to a website that attempts to download malware onto their computer or mobile device.

PREVENTIONS And Solutions

    Use ad blockers
  • •One of the most effective ways to prevent malvertising is to use an ad blocker that can block malicious ads from being displayed on websites. Popular ad blockers include uBlock Origin, AdBlock Plus, and Privacy Badger.
    Use anti-malware software
  • • Use anti-malware software that includes ad-blocking capabilities and can detect and remove malware that may be downloaded through malicious ads.
    Keep software and operating systems up to date
  • • Regularly update software and operating systems with the latest security patches and fixes to ensure that known vulnerabilities are patched and cannot be exploited by malvertising.
